\subsubsection*{Problem 3.a.}

By definition, we have
\begin{align*}
  Q =  (1-2\eps)\begin{pmatrix}
    1 & -\tau \\
    -\tau & \tau^2
  \end{pmatrix}
            + \eps \begin{pmatrix}
              1 & \tau \\
              \tau & \tau^2
            \end{pmatrix}
                     + \eps \begin{pmatrix}
                       0 & 0 \\
                       0 & 1
                     \end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}

We set $\eps = \tau^2$, then
\begin{align*}
  Q &=  (1-2\tau^2)\begin{pmatrix}
    1 & -\tau \\
    -\tau & \tau^2
  \end{pmatrix}
            + \tau^2 \begin{pmatrix}
              1 & \tau \\
              \tau & \tau^2
            \end{pmatrix}
                     + \tau^2 \begin{pmatrix}
                       0 & 0 \\
                       0 & 1
                     \end{pmatrix} \\
    &= \begin{pmatrix}
      1-\tau^2 & 3\tau^3 - r \\
      3\tau^3 - r & -\tau^4 + 2\tau^2
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}

Use wolfram alpha to find the $\Qinv$, we have
\begin{align*}
  \Qinv = \frac{1}{8r^4 - 3r^2 -1} \begin{pmatrix}
    \tau^2(\tau^2 - 2) & r(3\tau^3 - 1) \\
    r(3\tau^3 - 1) & \tau^2 -  1
  \end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}
